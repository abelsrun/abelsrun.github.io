Tasmania, Our Home

At Abels Run, we are passionate about reconnecting Tasmanian youth with the outdoors. Inspired by the breathtaking beauty of Tasmania, our mission is to create opportunities for young people to explore, learn, and thrive in the natural environment. Through education, support, and meaningful services, we aim to foster a love for nature and a commitment to its preservation. Find out more about our story and our mission below.

Tasmania, Our Playground

Our educational workshops are designed to re-engage youth in learning about Tasmania’s unique animals, geography, and cultural heritage. These workshops will be offered to schools, learning clubs, and after-school programs. Rather than traditional direct instruction learning, our approach emphasizes inquiry based hands-on learning with a focus on enjoyable and rewarding experiences.

Our Mission

"Abel's Run aims to re-engage Tasmanian youth with their natural environment by providing education and support, collaborating primarily with local companies for Tasmania’s benefit."

We aim to reconnect young Tasmanians with their state's natural environment by offering education, support, and services that may be lacking at home or in school. Reflecting on our own youth, we observed that many peers had limited knowledge of Tasmania's renowned natural wonders due to insufficient support in experiencing them.

Given our backgrounds, we recognize the necessity of digital literacy in a rapidly advancing technological world. As we stand at the dawn of the digital revolution, with its full impact still unfolding, even senior academics and industry leaders are uncertain about the future of technology. Yet, young people are expected to navigate this landscape without adequate support.

Our overarching purpose is to enrich our communities by focusing on the future—the children of Tasmania. We plan to achieve this by collaborating primarily with Tasmanian organizations, ensuring this initiative is created by the people of Tasmania for the benefit of young Tasmanians. Evidence indicates that excessive exposure to technology negatively affects our youth. Our aim is to educate them while gently shifting their focus away from a technology-driven world, reintroducing them to the healing and calming power of nature—something Tasmania is rich in and that is known to benefit mental well-being.

Our History

The ideology behind Abels Run began to take shape in late 2021, when our founder initiated a fundraising event through Cancer Council's "Do It for Cancer" program. The inspiration for this endeavour stemmed from his deep love for Tasmania and its natural beauty, a passion nurtured through his pursuits as a mountaineer with the ambitious goal of summiting the state’s 158 Abel mountains.

However, the driving force behind this mission was an act of love and support for his terminally ill grandmother, Sheila Jackson, who was battling multiple health conditions, including cancer. Sheila’s struggle became a source of profound motivation for Noah, pushing him to strive harder to achieve this physically demanding goal. Sadly, Sheila passed away in early 2022, but she left Noah with her wedding ring, which he carries with him every day as a symbol of her.

To date, Abels Run has raised over $7,000 for the Cancer Council, a testament to Noah's dedication and the community's support. Looking ahead, Noah plans to complete his remaining Abels in a 3-month period during 2025, in an event titled "Abels Run: The Final Push." The objective of this final challenge is to raise $15,800 for cancer research in Tasmania, while summiting the estimated 58 remaining mountains—some of which will involve expeditions lasting over 8 days.This event is intended to serve as the launchpad for his broader program, with the goal of establishing Abels Run as a not-for-profit charity.

Our Workshops

For committed organisations, we have secured roughly 21 workshops, with The Smith Family offering an additional 18 workshop program pending success. We want to reach all areas of Tasmania, focusing on different learning items pending regional location and age groups.

For younger student workshops we aim to focus on Tasmania’s unique animals, geography, and cultural heritage. Emphasizing hands-on, inquiry-based learning rather than traditional direct instruction. As we move to secondary offerings, we will gently shift toward digital literacy, as they face the real threat of device and social media addiction.

The workshops will be held in schools, learning clubs, and after-school programs. For students that are interested, Abel's Run will offer bushwalking experiences. These excursions will be fully supported, providing transportation, documentation, gear, food, and social services. The walks will vary from day trips to multi-night adventures, all led by experienced and trained staff. Logical thinking plays a key part in selecting our walks. Senior students will be able to access overnight bushwalks, whereas primary students will be able to access day walks and overnight camping. We aim to run multiple of these services during our initial year.

Abel's Run will operate solely within Tasmania, statewide. Our activities will primarily be conducted in educational settings (schools, learning clubs, and holiday care programs) and the state's national parks. The project is deeply rooted in Tasmania, and our focus is to keep it in Tasmania, always.

Expected Outcomes

Increased Engagement with Nature.

By shifting students away from technology and providing an alternative experience in our natural environment, our participants are expected to improve mental wellbeing and develop a deeper appreciation for Tasmania's natural heritage.

Educational Enrichment.

The hands-on learning experiences provided through the workshops will enrich the students' knowledge of Tasmania’s environment and cultural heritage, fostering a sense of pride and connection to their home state.

Future Technological Literacy.

We don’t want to be “those guys” that go into classrooms to tell kids to get off their phone. Instead, we aim to introduce students about the negative habits they might have, the dangers of the industries they support, and what the future of technology looks like. We hope to send them home with a new mindset on what they spend their free time on.

Community Building.

As our identified problem is one most understand, we hope to strengthen community ties and build a supportive network for the youth of Tasmania. Collaboration with local businesses and involving the community in the program, we benefit all involved.
